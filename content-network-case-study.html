Case Study

Content Network

I defined the UX and product architecture for Western Union’s Content Network — a personalisation engine that transforms a single-use transfer utility into an engagement platform. By architecting the recommendation engine’s decisioning logic, validating the strategy through three customer journey segments, and establishing a guardrail metrics framework, I gave Western Union the infrastructure to deliver relevant content across 200+ markets at scale.

Content Ecosystem

The post-transaction surface — one of the highest-value content placements in the ecosystem. After a successful transfer, the recommendation engine surfaces next best actions, loyalty content, and relevant deals based on the user’s corridor and behavioural signals.

Sender Journey

Content surfaced within the core transfer flow: promotional offers at the estimate screen, loyalty enrolment as a next best action post-transfer, and educational content embedded within the receipt page — without disrupting the primary conversion path.

Delivery Architecture

The distribution ecosystem: content types (Deals, Promotional, Educational, Editorial, Community) processed through a decisioning layer and distributed across App, Web, Email, and DOOH channels into specific placement slots.

Process

The project began by mapping 4 compounding problems: customers unaware of the broader WU value proposition; content not driving lifecycle engagement; wrong content at wrong moments; and receivers as a completely untapped growth loop. A 6-week discovery phase validated these through data analysis and stakeholder interviews across Product, Marketing, Data Science, and Engineering.

Three strategic decisions defined the approach: a recommendation engine (not editorial scheduling) so personalisation could scale across 200+ markets without a per-market content team; receivers as a growth engine by designing content pathways to convert them into senders; and guardrail metrics as a mandatory shipping gate — every content placement must define a core-transfer threshold before launch, protecting the business fundamentals while validating engagement hypotheses.

The Canada MVP tested content on homepage and receipt slots using rule-based targeting, capturing behavioural signals for the recommendation engine, and integrating the first affiliate partner. Each step was designed to build the data foundation for ML-based personalisation in subsequent phases.
