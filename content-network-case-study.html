The strategic view — key decisions, contribution & outcomes
The Problem
Western Union was a single-use transfer utility with no engagement engine between transactions

Rising customer acquisition costs made retention critical, yet WU had no personalized content capability. Customers sent money and left — with no mechanism to educate them on loyalty, cross-sell adjacent services, or build habitual usage. Surface areas across app, web, and email were sitting idle.

My Role & Contribution
Lead UX & Product Strategy — full ownership from discovery to MVP

Defined the customer experience architecture for Content Network: the recommendation engine's decisioning logic, the 6 content type framework, the 3-persona customer journey validation (Will, Bharath, Stella), and the guardrail metrics system. Partnered with Product, Engineering, Data Science, Marketing, and Affiliates across two regions.

Three Key Strategic Decisions
1
Recommendation engine, not editorial scheduling: Architected personalization around a dynamic decisioning layer that routes content based on behavioral signals — the only approach that could scale across 200+ markets without a per-market content team.
2
Receivers as an untapped growth engine: Broke from WU's sender-centric model to design content pathways for receivers. With 90% of senders sending to 1–2 recipients in the same country, corridor-specific content for receivers is the highest-relevance, lowest-cost acquisition channel available.
3
Guardrail metrics as a mandatory shipping gate: Mandated that every content placement define a transfer-rate guardrail before launch. This gave Engineering and Product confidence to test engagement aggressively without risking core conversion — and it prevented the classic engagement-vs-conversion trade-off.

01 — The Problem

Why the Transaction-Only Model Was Unsustainable

Four compounding problems were eroding Western Union's ability to build lasting customer relationships — and creating an urgent strategic opportunity.

Problem 01
Hidden ecosystem value

Customers were unaware of services beyond money transfer — loyalty, bill pay, digital wallets — leading to chronic underutilisation and low LTV despite a broad product portfolio.

↗ Content is not being used to introduce, educate, or activate cross-product features.
Problem 02
No lifecycle engagement

Content wasn't relevant or timely — no personalised nudges tied to customer lifecycle stages, resulting in high drop-off after a single transaction and low repeat usage.

↗ Lack of next best actions, benefit unlocks, and product cross-sells tied to behaviour.
Problem 03
Wrong content at wrong moments

We weren't surfacing feature benefits at the right time — content was generic, poorly timed, and disconnected from what users were actually doing in the app at that moment.

↗ Missed adoption opportunities; reduced perceived value of the ecosystem.
Problem 04
Receivers: the ignored growth loop

WU's content and product focus was 100% sender-centric, missing the receiver segment entirely — a substantial and captive audience with clear upsell potential.

↗ Receivers represent an untapped growth loop — content isn't tailored to convert them into active senders.
02 — Definition

What is Content Network?

A strategic product capability that delivers personalised, context-aware content across key touchpoints — transforming passive experiences into value-driven journeys.

The Vision

"A world where content drives habit, not just clicks — where data powers timely, personalised experiences across our ecosystem."

By combining relevant messaging, diverse placements, and data-driven recommendations, it transforms passive experiences into engaging, value-driven journeys — fostering stronger customer relationships beyond the transaction.

The Recommendation Engine

The intelligence layer that powers personalisation across all content surfaces. A dynamic decisioning system that determines which content to show, to which users, at what time, in which placement — based on behavioural, contextual, and business rules.

04 — Customer Journey Design

Three Journeys. Three Segments. One System.

Customer stories validated that a single Content Network, with the right decisioning, could serve radically different user needs — without bespoke builds per segment.

Will
First-time sender · US to Mexico
Acquisition + Activation

Recently relocated from Mexico to New York. Needs to set up recurring transfers to his father and find retail payout options. He doesn't know WU's loyalty programme exists.

Content Network outcome
Promotional content on the estimate screen highlights $0 transfer fee. Post-transfer, the recommendation engine surfaces loyalty enrolment as a Next Best Action — saving Will money on future transactions and increasing LTV for WU.
Bharath
Frequent sender · India to US · Price-sensitive
Engagement + Education

Student at NYU, sends money home regularly. Uses WU for price comparisons but has no brand loyalty. Prioritises cost and is keen on building US credit history as a new immigrant.

Content Network outcome
Educational content about Upwardli Credit Builder appears during app visits. Monthly notifications (credit score alerts, blog content) keep Bharath engaged between transfers — turning a price-comparison visit into a habitual relationship.
Stella
Loyal customer · Canada · Rewards engaged
Retention + Monetisation

Avid WU customer in Canada. Shops at brands she loves. After sending money, discovers a Deals section she wasn't aware of and signs up for Rewards.

Content Network outcome
Deal saved. A reminder notification drives her back to the app. She redeems the deal and shares it with a friend in her WU directory — creating a viral, word-of-mouth acquisition loop that required zero paid media spend.
05 — Competitive Strategy

Why Western Union Is Positioned to Win

Competitors use affiliate marketing for acquisition. WU has the infrastructure to go further — building a content network that lives where users already transact.

Competitors (Remitly, Wise, MoneyGram)
  • ✕ Rely on third-party affiliate banners and links
  • ✕ One-off conversions, no engagement ownership
  • ✕ External content, not contextual to the transfer journey
  • ✕ No behavioural personalisation at content level
Western Union (Content Network)
  • ✓ Content lives inside the product — contextual, not external
  • ✓ 200+ country presence enables localised content at global scale
  • ✓ Behavioural + transactional data powers personalisation at user level
  • ✓ Retail footprint provides a migration path from physical to digital — exclusive advantage
→

None of our competitors have a Content Network that seamlessly integrates personalised content and deals into the core CX. This is our competitive advantage — a flywheel of engagement, relevance, and habit formation that second-mover competitors cannot easily replicate.

07 — MVP & Rollout

From Vision to Impact — Five Steps

Canada as test ground. Five sequential steps to validate hypotheses before committing to global investment — a deliberate, low-risk validation strategy.

01
Test content on dedicated slots — Homepage + Receipt (Canada E1 & US R3)

Desktop web, mobile web, and app. Validate impression volume, engagement rate, and CTR across the two highest-traffic surfaces before expanding placement inventory.

02
Use rule-based targeting to reach the right customers

Start with crude but precise rules (new users, post-first transfer, loyalty-enrolled vs. not). This establishes baseline targeting accuracy before investing in ML-based personalisation.

03
Capture behavioural signals into internal data systems

Every interaction (impression, click, save, dismiss) feeds the recommendation engine. This is the data flywheel that makes personalisation increasingly accurate over time.

04
Build internal CMS with campaign workflows and localisation

Enable content teams to create, schedule, and localise content independently of Engineering. CMS with business rules engine, translation support, and A/B testing capability.

05
Integrate affiliate partner to drive curated deals engagement

First external partnership integrated into the placement ecosystem — validating the deals and offers content type and establishing the monetisation foundation for future partnerships.

08 — Metrics Framework

Four Objectives. Measured at Every Layer.

The measurement framework maps each objective to primary metrics, supporting metrics, and placement-level guardrails — ensuring content never cannibalises core transfer performance.

Reach
Network Size
  • Total impressions (daily / weekly / monthly)
  • Active content placements
  • % of traffic covered by content
Engagement
Depth of Interaction
  • Click-through rate (CTR)
  • Average time spent on content
  • Scroll depth / % viewed
Monetisation
Conversion
  • Conversion rate (CVR) post-click
  • Revenue influenced by content
  • Downstream actions (sign-up, send)
Habituation
Return & Frequency
  • Return visits within 7 / 14 / 30 days
  • % of users engaging multiple times
  • Content-driven session frequency
