01 — The Challenge

Moving Money Shouldn't
Feel Like Moving Mountains

International remittances are inherently complex — currency conversion, compliance requirements, varied delivery methods. Our challenge was to abstract that complexity without sacrificing transparency or trust.

🌐

Corridor Complexity

Each country corridor has unique rules: available delivery methods, required fields, and compliance checks vary dramatically across 200+ destinations.

💳

Payment Fragmentation

Users need to choose from bank accounts, PayID, debit cards, and in-store deposits — each with different fees, speeds, and trust signals.

⚡

Drop-off at Transfer

Analytics showed 43% of users abandoned the flow at the amount entry screen — they couldn't quickly understand fees, rates, or total cost.

🔒

Security vs. Speed

Regulatory requirements (AML/KYC) demand friction points, but every added step reduced conversion. We needed smart progressive disclosure.

👥

Repeat Senders

68% of transactions go to previously-used recipients, yet the existing flow treated every transfer as new — wasting users' time on familiar paths.

📱

Native Experience Gap

The existing hybrid app felt webview-heavy. Users expected banking-app-level polish with instant feedback and fluid transitions.

02 — Strategic Approach

Design Decisions
That Shaped the Product

As UX lead, I drove four foundational decisions that defined the architecture of this experience.

Architecture Progressive Disclosure Engine

Instead of a linear wizard, we built a single-screen "estimate view" that progressively reveals complexity. Users see amount + rate immediately, with payment/delivery expanding contextually. This reduced perceived steps from 7 to 3.

Trust Transparency-First Pricing

Fee, exchange rate, and estimated delivery time visible at all times via a persistent bottom container. Users never wonder "what will this cost?" — the answer updates in real-time as they type.

Speed Contact-Centric Model

We flipped the mental model from "fill a form" to "send to a person." Saved contacts surface immediately with pre-populated corridors, reducing repeat-sender flows to 2 taps + amount entry.

Safety Intelligent Security Touchpoints

Security notices and fraud warnings appear contextually (new recipients, large amounts, unusual corridors) rather than as blanket gates. This preserved conversion while meeting compliance requirements.

1

Choose Currency & Corridor

The journey begins with destination selection. We prioritized recently-used countries at the top, with intelligent search that handles both country names and currency codes.

  • Recent countries appear first — 72% of sends go to top 3 corridors per user
  • Search handles typos ("Phillippines" → shows error state with suggestion)
  • Flag icons provide instant visual recognition for frequent senders
  • Selected state (checkmark) gives clear confirmation before advancing
2

The Estimate Screen — Our Core Innovation

This single screen is the heart of the experience. It combines amount entry, real-time conversion, payment/delivery selection, and fee transparency into one cohesive view.

  • Dual-currency input: type in sender OR receiver currency
  • Live exchange rate chip ("1 AUD = 38.7819 PHP") builds trust
  • Payment & delivery cards update fees dynamically
  • Persistent bottom sheet: fee + total + delivery time always visible
  • Yellow "Continue" CTA only activates when all fields are valid
3

Payment & Delivery Method Selection

We designed a "Saved vs. New" toggle pattern that rewards returning users while keeping new method setup accessible. Each option shows fee + speed upfront.

  • Saved tab shows previously used methods for 1-tap selection
  • New tab presents all available options with fee/speed comparison
  • PayID integration includes educational modal for first-time users
  • "Should arrive in minutes" vs "1-2 business days" — speed as a key differentiator
4

Delivery Method & Multiple Variants

The send methods screen adapts based on corridor availability. Users see a clear comparison of AUD account, bank transfers, PayID, debit cards, and in-store deposits — each with contextual fees.

  • 7 distinct payment method variations designed and validated
  • Bottom drawer pattern for PayID education without losing context
  • Promo code field positioned strategically below payment selection
  • Consistent pricing container anchored at bottom across all variants
5

Recipient Selection & Contact Intelligence

For returning users, we surface saved contacts with their last-used corridor. The "Who are you sending money to?" heading uses human language to reduce cognitive load.

  • Contacts show name + country flag for instant recognition
  • Search supports name, alias, or mobile number
  • "New payment" CTA always accessible for first-time recipients
  • Single-contact view auto-advances to estimate screen

04 — Design Principles

Guiding Principles

Six principles that governed every design decision in this project.

01
Show the Cost Immediately
Users should never have to guess what they'll pay. Fees, rates, and totals are visible from the moment an amount is entered.
02
Reward Returning Users
68% of transfers go to saved contacts. The experience should get faster with each use through saved methods, pre-filled corridors, and contact shortcuts.
03
Progressive Security
Compliance gates appear only when contextually relevant — new recipients, unusual patterns, high-value transfers. Not as blanket friction for all users.
04
Single-Screen Mental Model
The estimate screen consolidates what was previously 4 screens into one progressive view. Users maintain context and can adjust without navigating backward.
05
Speed as Differentiator
Delivery time is surfaced alongside every method choice. "Should arrive in minutes" isn't buried — it's a primary decision factor displayed upfront.
06
Human Language, Not Jargon
"Who are you sending money to?" instead of "Select recipient." "They get" instead of "Receive amount." Every label was written for humans, not bankers.
05 — Process

How We Got Here

A dual-track approach: continuous discovery alongside iterative delivery.

Week 1–2
Landscape Audit & Data Mining
Analyzed 2.4M transfer sessions across top 10 corridors. Identified 3 critical drop-off points: currency selection (12%), amount entry (43%), and payment method (28%). Benchmarked against Wise, Remitly, and banking apps.
Week 3–4
User Research — 24 Interviews
Moderated sessions with diaspora communities in Australia. Key insight: users think in "person + amount" not "corridor + method." Trust signals (exchange rate visibility) ranked #1 in card sorts.
Week 5–7
Concept Exploration & Architecture
Explored 4 architectural approaches. Settled on "progressive estimate" model after testing with 8 participants. Built component-driven design system (Nexus DS) in parallel for consistency.
Week 8–10
Hi-Fi Design & Usability Testing
3 rounds of unmoderated testing (n=45). Iterated on: keyboard dismissal, bottom sheet height, CTA states, and security notice placement. Achieved 92% task completion in final round.
Week 11–14
Engineering Handoff & Design QA
Delivered pixel-perfect specs via Figma Dev Mode. Partnered with native iOS/Android teams on motion design (shared element transitions, skeleton states). 142 unique screens across all variants and states.
Week 15+
Staged Rollout & Measurement
A/B tested with 5% → 20% → 100% rollout in Australian market. Monitored completion rates, time-to-send, NPS delta, and support ticket volume.
06 — Outcomes

Measurable Impact

Results from the first 8 weeks of full rollout in the Australian market.

+34%
Completion Rate

-52%

08 — Key Learnings

What I'd Tell My
Next Project Self

🎯

Measure the Right Funnel

Screen-level drop-off misled us initially. The real insight came from measuring "intent to completion" — users who entered an amount and didn't finish. That's where the real opportunity lived.

🔄

Design for the Return Visit

First-time UX got all the attention in reviews. But when 68% of revenue comes from repeat senders, the 2nd-use experience is the product. Optimize for the majority, not the onboarding.

🤝

Compliance as Co-Design Partner

Bringing legal/compliance into design sprints (not reviews) unlocked contextual security patterns that satisfied regulators AND users. They became advocates for progressive disclosure.
