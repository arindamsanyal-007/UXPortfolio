UX Baseline

I established the global design reference for Western Union’s Send Money experience — a validated, screen-level baseline that any team worldwide could build against. By transforming quality from subjective critique into an objective gate, I enabled 200+ market teams to ship consistently at scale, cutting corridor delivery time in half and eliminating critical accessibility failures.

Entry Pattern

Payments Hub baseline: contact-centric navigation that establishes how users initiate transfers. Every market adapts from this reference, never redesigning the entry pattern independently.

Selection Pattern

Country & currency selection and delivery method selection — both defined with 4 states each (default, active, selected, error) and validated for keyboard navigation, screen reader labels, and 44pt touch targets.

Complete Flow

The full bank account transfer baseline — 19 validated screens covering entry through confirmation and error states. The definitive reference for engineering handoff and market adaptation.

Process

The baseline began with a systematic audit of all live user flows across markets, identifying inconsistencies and measuring completion rates by flow. This revealed that the same patterns were being designed, tested, and documented multiple times across different squads — accumulating drift with every release.

I established three strategic principles: Design once, adapt everywhere (hub-and-spoke model); Quality as a gate, not a review (objective checklist embedded in baseline); Centralised ownership, distributed execution (central team owns pattern decisions, squads execute). Instruction pages embedded in Figma and pre-configured page templates operationalized these principles at the file level, making compliance structural rather than behavioural.
